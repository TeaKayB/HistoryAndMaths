Join a Growing Cross-Curricular Community

HaMiEN functions entirely through the shared insights of its members: it's all about developing a community of practitioners and researchers with a range of experience: whether you're asking quick questions about history in maths classrooms, sharing an article you've stumbled upon about an example of maths in a historical setting, or asking for feedback on a resource you've created, your voice belongs in the discussion.

PRIMARY COMMUNITY HUB

Subscribe to the Jiscmail Discussion List

Our public email list is the central heartbeat of the network. It is entirely free to join, public, and securely archived online for continuous reference.

What to expect when you subscribe:

  • Become part of a growing network of education experts and subject enthusiasts who are all interested in exploring how Mathematics and History can enrich each other in the classroom;
  • Notifications for network-relevant events, appearances, publications, and resources;
  • A low-volume, zero-spam, not-for-profit feed dedicated to facilitating connections and sharing resources.

How to Take Part

Here are just some of the things that we'd love to see being shared via the discussion list:

  • Recommend a resource: Have you read an intriguing paper, delivered a fab classroom activity, watched a video, or read a book that might be of interest to HaMiEN's members? Don't keep it to yourself! If they're really good we'll add them to the Resources library too.
  • Let us know about an event or activity: Have you spotted a conference, author talk, or other upcoming event? Tell the communiity! We'll add any great ones on the Events calendar if we hear about them in time.
  • Share your experiences: Have you been to an event, used a resource, read a book, stumbled upon an opportunity to explore maths at a museum (etc)? Reviews and musings on network-relevant topics are always welcome.
  • Ask a question: Are you looking for inspiration or feedback, or do you have an idea that you'd like to collaborate on? That's exactly what the HaMiEN is for.

Anything HaMiEN-relevant can be shared via the Jiscmail list, but do remember that anything you post could be found by anyone with an internet connection, whether they're a member of the network or not.

If you'd like to share or discuss something privately, you can always get in touch.

Beyond the Discussion List

Help us build the network, and promote History + Maths chat at the same time:

  • Post network-relevant things via your socials: Spotted some historical maths (or mathematical history) out in the wild? Take a photo and share it with your followers!
  • Use relevant hashtags: Posting about History and Maths? Include #History and #Maths!. Was it in a museum? Add #MathsInMuseums too!
  • Share resources: Found something great in our resource bank. Share it with your followers, and let them know where you found it.
  • Mention the HaMiEN: Tell people that the HaMiEN exists, point them towards historyand.mathsy.space, and include #HaMiEN whenever you post something that other members might be interested in.
